# Billing FAQ

## How Does Billing Work?

CloudSync Pro uses a subscription-based billing model. You are charged at the beginning of each billing cycle based on your selected plan. All charges are prorated when you upgrade or downgrade mid-cycle. Your billing cycle starts on the day you first subscribe and renews on the same date each month (or year, for annual plans).

## What Is My Billing Cycle?

Your billing cycle depends on the plan frequency you selected at signup:

- **Monthly plans**: Billed on the same date each month. If you subscribed on March 15, your next charge is April 15.
- **Annual plans**: Billed once per year on your subscription anniversary date. Annual plans receive a 20% discount compared to monthly billing.

You can view your current billing cycle dates by navigating to **Settings > Billing > Subscription Details**.

## What Payment Methods Are Accepted?

CloudSync Pro accepts the following payment methods:

- Visa, Mastercard, American Express, and Discover credit/debit cards
- ACH bank transfers (US customers only, Pro and Enterprise plans)
- Wire transfers (Enterprise plans only, minimum $5,000 annual commitment)
- PayPal (monthly plans only)

## When Will I Be Charged?

Charges are processed at 12:00 AM UTC on your billing date. If your payment fails, we retry automatically on days 1, 3, and 7 after the initial failure. If all retries fail, your account is downgraded to the Free plan on day 10.

## How Do I View My Current Plan and Charges?

Go to **Settings > Billing > Overview**. This page shows your current plan, next billing date, payment method on file, and a summary of upcoming charges including any add-ons or overages.

## Are There Any Hidden Fees?

No. CloudSync Pro does not charge setup fees, cancellation fees, or overage fees without prior notification. If your usage exceeds plan limits (e.g., storage or API calls), you will receive a warning email at 80% and 95% usage before any overage charges apply. Overage rates are listed on the pricing page at cloudsyncpro.com/pricing.
